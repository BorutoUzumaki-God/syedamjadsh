Profound Point On Scheduling Heuristics
(automotive Context)

Pure optimization models can't handle the messy realities of the shop floor: unpredictable defects, late supplier trucks, machine breakdowns. That's why heuristics matter.

Heuristics in automotive scheduling act as practical decision rules, such as:

Always Prioritize Jobs That Unblock The Most Downstream Operations.

Batch By Paint Colour Or Trim Variant Until Changeover Cost Exceeds Idle Cost.

Resequencing Cars To Keep Takt Flow Stable Even When One Sku Fails Qc.

These heuristics bring speed, resilience, and explainability. Giving planners a way to adjust schedules dynamically without waiting for an optimizer to rerun a 6-hour simulation.
